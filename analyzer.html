How each metric is calculated:
PTI = (payment × periods per year ÷ 12 + existing auto loan payment) ÷ monthly net income  ·  Adjusts automatically for bi-weekly, semi-monthly, and monthly pay frequencies
Residual income = monthly net income − housing expense − our monthly payment equivalent − existing auto loan payment
DP coverage = actual day-1 cash (down payment minus any deferred amount) ÷ monthly payment equivalent
Stability (green) = 12+ months on job AND 6+ months at residence  ·  Amber = 6–11 months on job OR 3–5 months at residence  ·  Red = under 6 months on job OR under 3 months at residence
Breakeven = (total dealer cash out − actual day-1 cash) ÷ payment, converted to months
Payment auto-calc = round(amortized(AF, term, 25% APR))  ·  Formula verified against NeoVerify across 9 quotes
State tax base = sale price + service fee ($699)  ·  Doc stamp on final AF at $0.35 per $100
Neo grade and credit score: informational only  ·  Government benefits = green (fixed income)  ·  0–1 repos = green (common in BHPH)  ·  Deferred DP: weekly customers only, must be collected within 7 days  ·  Previously repoed with EZ AUTO = hard Deny  ·  Approve requires: 0 reds, ≤2 ambers, breakeven ≤10 months, PTI <20%, residual ≥$800/mo  ·  Breakeven ceiling is dynamic: 16 months for strong profiles (0 reds, ≤2 ambers), 14 months for moderate profiles (0 reds, 3–4 ambers), 12 months when 1 red flag present  ·  DP coverage of 3+ months is a positive modifier and explicitly noted in Lean Approve reasoning
Co-signer policy
Two gates required — both must pass before co-signer income counts toward any calculation. Gate 1: matching POR for both primary and co-signer showing same address. Gate 2: verified relationship.
Direct relative — Spouse, Parent, Child, or Sibling only. Qualifies automatically once relationship is selected and both PORs confirmed. No other relationships permitted.
Domestic partner — Must produce at least 2 joint financial documents: joint lease/mortgage, joint bank account, joint utility bill, or other shared financial obligation. Boyfriend/girlfriend without documented financial entanglement do not qualify.
PTI hard deny threshold — When a qualified co-signer is present, PTI hard deny applies to combined household PTI, not primary-only PTI. A 19-year-old with 33% individual PTI whose parent co-signs may have 8% combined PTI — that deal qualifies. Without a co-signer, primary PTI over 30% is always a hard deny.
What co-signer income does NOT affect — Breakeven calculation, dynamic ceiling, minimum DP formula, deal economics. Those are structural and do not change regardless of co-signer income.
Co-signer open auto loan — Added to combined PTI and subtracted from combined residual income. A co-signer with a $350/mo existing auto loan reduces the household capacity — this is always factored in.
Co-signer flags count — Co-signer job stability, income type, and existing auto loan generate flags that count toward the amber/red totals driving the verdict ceiling. A weak co-signer profile adds risk, not just income.
